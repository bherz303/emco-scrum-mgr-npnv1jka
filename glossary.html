Groups — Where Pulses Live
Inbox

New, untriaged items land here. Triaged at sync: owner, next action, due date, priority assigned, then moved out. Nothing should sit untriaged past 5 days.

Today

What's being worked on today. If it's not being touched today, it doesn't belong here.

This Week

Work planned for this week, each item carrying the specific day it's planned for. Moves to Today when that day arrives.

Later / Deferred

Not this week. Every item here must carry a future Monday date — that date is what triggers the automation pulling it back to Inbox before the next sync.

Hold for Sync Up

Where "Done" items rest until reviewed at the next sync-up.

Done

Completed, or sufficiently progressed work that has no future follow-up. The status for these pulses is Synced, marking that they won't be picked back up.

Quarterly Goals

The reference group where QG (Quarterly Goal) main pulses live, so they're easy to find. Note: a duplicate live QG pulse lives in the Today and This Week groups for active updates.

Core Vocabulary
DLOW (Daily Level of Work)

The Fibonacci-scale point value on a Today item estimating how much of the day it takes. Must always be a Fibonacci number.

WLOW (Weekly Level of Work)

A cumulative point total for the week. Does not have to be Fibonacci — it's a sum, not an estimate.

Fibonacci LOW scale

The relative-sizing scale used instead of clock-time estimates: 1 = 15 min or less · 2 = 30–45 min · 3 = 1–2 hrs · 5 = 3–4 hrs · 8 = 5–6 hrs · 13 = 7–8 hrs · 21 = 9–12 hrs.

Target DLOW

The healthy daily load per person: 15–20 points. Above 40 in Today means overloaded — triage.

QG (Quarterly Goal) pulse

A pulse representing one of the "big rocks" for the quarter. Also called a QG Live pulse when actively being worked.

QG Value

The point value assigned to a QG pulse, usually matching its Weekly LOW. Keeps the most important work visible above day-to-day noise.

Velocity

Weekly LOW plus QG Value for the week — the team's pace. Reviewed every sync.

Velocity Check pulse

A tracking pulse (not a worked task) used to review pace at sync. Allowed a DLOW of 0 since it isn't real work — that exception applies only to this specific pulse. Often split into one copy per person (BM, FM, Flex manager) so each tracks their own pace; that's legitimate, not a duplicate.

Happiness pulse

One pulse per person that, when completed, makes them happy for the week. Should be visible in Today or This Week, not buried. Note: this typically should be tied to a high-value task, or a task that needs to get done in order to get back on track with more valuable tasks.

Project column

The tag identifying what category of work a pulse belongs to (e.g., QG, DB Pulse, Delegation Pulse). QG and DB Pulse items must always be tagged; overall at least half the active board should be tagged.

DB Pulse

A Roundtable "DB" (database) item — company-wide tracked items that flow through the branch board's Inbox. Often appears as one copy per assignee before triage.

Delegation Pulse

A pulse tagged as a delegated task, same per-assignee-copy pattern as DB Pulses while sitting untriaged in Inbox.

Sales block

A recurring scheduled pulse for a sales activity (e.g., "Monday PM Sales Block"). Multiple sales-block pulses on different days are intentional, not duplicates.

Damage pulse / damage claim

A pulse tracking a customer damage claim. Excluded from certain scoring checks (e.g., Definition of Done) since these often stay open past a single sync cycle by nature.

Sub-items

Nested detail rows under a pulse. Sub-item content (names, amounts, status notes, dates) counts as valid context, satisfying the Updates-with-Context criterion even without a top-level update post.

Automations

Monday's status-driven behavior: changing an item's status (e.g., to Today, This Week, Deferred, Done) automatically moves it to the matching group and, for Today, stamps today's date. Dragging a pulse between groups manually bypasses this and is what the Automations criterion grades against.

Scoring & Grading
Board Hygiene

The 12-criterion, 60-point rubric that grades the state of the board itself (LOW values, titles, dates, duplicates, automations, etc.).

Meeting Management

The 8-item checklist grading how a sync-up itself was run (agenda flow, timing, tone, etc.), scored only on a Full Eval.

Full Eval

A scorecard read that includes both Board Hygiene and Meeting Management (i.e., the grader observed a live sync-up, not just the board).

Board-only read

A scorecard read scoring just Board Hygiene, no live sync observed.

Scorecard read

Any single graded pass against a branch board, logged to the dashboard with a date, score, and grade.

Green / Yellow / Red

The three-tier scoring color per individual criterion: Green (5 pts, nailed it), Yellow (3 pts, partially there), Red (1 pt, not in place — bonus criteria score 0 on Red instead of 1).

Grade Scale (A–F)

The overall score-to-letter mapping: A = 90–100% (Excellent), B = 80–89% (Strong), C = 70–79% (Needs work), D = 60–69% (Falling behind), F = below 60% (Board needs reset).

Rubric version

The dated version of the scoring rubric (e.g., v1.3) tracked so historical scores stay interpretable even after the rubric is corrected.

Certification
Certification

The status a manager earns after passing all three training checkpoints, scoring 90%+ on Board Hygiene two weeks running, and leading a mock sync that scores Green on Meeting Management.

Recertification

Repeating the certification bar (90%+ on two reads running) after a decertification.

Decertification

Losing certified status, triggered by a single read below 60% (F) or two consecutive monthly reads both below 90% (A).

Maintenance read

The once-a-month formal, logged scorecard read that keeps a certified board's status current.

Roles
RM

Regional Manager.

BM

Branch Manager (owns the branch board being graded).

FM

Field Manager.

Flex

Flex Manager (covers across roles/branches as needed).
