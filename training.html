Stage 1 · Day 1–2

Learn the Board

Understand what the board is, how work flows through it, and the vocabulary behind the scorecard.

Why we use a Scrum board

Your Monday board is your hammer and chisel. There is only so much time in a day, and the board is how we decide what actually gets worked, who owns it, and whether we're moving fast enough. A clean board isn't busywork — it's the difference between reacting to your week and running it.

The groups and how work flows

Work moves through groups in a predictable cycle. Learn this flow — most board problems come from items sitting in the wrong group.

  • Inbox — New, untriaged items land here. At sync: decide owner, next action, due date, priority. Nothing should rot here past 5 days.
  • Today — What you are working on today. If you're not touching it today, it doesn't belong here.
  • This Week — Work happening this week, each item carrying the specific day. When that day arrives, it moves to Today.
  • Later / Deferred — Not this week. Every deferred item must carry a future Monday date — that triggers the automation to pull it back into Inbox before your next sync.
  • Hold for Sync Up — Where "Done" items rest until reviewed at the next sync.
  • Done — Completed or sufficiently progressed for the week.

This flow is automated, not manual. Changing an item's status is what moves it — you never drag a pulse between groups. Setting status to Today, This Week, Deferred, or Done automatically relocates the item, and status → Today also stamps today's date. Drag-and-drop bypasses this and is exactly what the Automations criterion is grading against.

Try it on the Mock Practice Board: change any item's status and watch it jump groups on its own.

The Fibonacci LOW scale

We're bad at estimating time, so we use relative sizing instead. Daily Level of Work (DLOW) uses the Fibonacci scale: 1 = 15 min or less · 2 = 30–45 min · 3 = 1–2 hrs · 5 = 3–4 hrs · 8 = 5–6 hrs · 13 = 7–8 hrs · 21 = 9–12 hrs.

Daily LOW (DLOW) must always be one of these numbers. Weekly LOW (WLOW) is a cumulative total — it does not have to be Fibonacci. Target DLOW is 15–20 per person per day. If Today adds up to 40+, you're overloaded — triage.

QG Value, Velocity, and the Happiness pulse

  • QG Value — The point value on your Quarterly Goal pulses (your "big rocks"), usually matching Weekly LOW for that goal. Keeps your most important work visible above day-to-day noise.
  • Velocity — WLOW plus QG Value gives your pace for the week. Reviewed every sync.
  • Happiness pulse — Every team member should have one pulse that, when completed, makes them happy for the week. Visible in Today or This Week.
✅ Stage 1 checkpoint
  1. Name the six groups and what belongs in each.
  2. What's the difference between DLOW and WLOW? Which one must be Fibonacci?
  3. Why must deferred items have a Monday date?
  4. What is your target DLOW per day?
Stage 2 · Day 3–5

Work the Board

Keep your board scoring Green against all 12 board hygiene criteria.

This stage maps directly to the scorecard. Each item below is what a grader looks for — learn to self-check before your board is ever graded.

The 10 core criteria (5 / 3 / 1 points each)

1. LOW Values — Every Today item has a valid Fibonacci DLOW.Self-check: scan Today — is any DLOW blank or non-Fibonacci (like 4 or 6)? One exception: the Velocity Check pulse is allowed DLOW 0 — it's a tracking pulse, not a worked task. That exception applies only to that pulse; a real task with DLOW 0 is still a miss.
2. Pulse Titles — Titles name the next action with enough context. Recognized shorthand ("ESP", "Dispatch", "Courtesy Texts") is fine.Self-check: would a teammate know what to do from the title alone?
3. Later Group Dates — Every deferred item has a future Monday date.Self-check: sort Deferred by date — any past dates or non-Mondays?
4. QG Pulses & Happiness Visible — At least one QG Live pulse is in Today/This Week, and a Happiness pulse is present in either.Self-check: are your QG pulses parked in Deferred where nobody sees them?
5. QG Value on QG Pulses — Your Quarterly Goal pulses have the QG Value column filled in.Self-check: any QG Live pulse with a blank QG Value?
6. Inbox Age — Nothing untriaged older than 5 days.Self-check: sort Inbox by created date — anything stale? Check every item, not just the first one you spot. Freshly dropped items (today or yesterday) are not a violation.
7. Project Column — QG and DB Pulse items are always tagged; overall at least half the active board is tagged.Self-check: are your QG and DB pulses labeled correctly?
8. Updates with Context — Critical ongoing-project pulses (QG, fleet repair/maintenance, customer issues, employee tracking) carry current progress notes. Simple task placeholders don't need updates.Self-check: does each customer issue and fleet pulse have a recent note on where it stands?
9. No Duplicates — One pulse per task.Note: multiple sales blocks on different days are intentional, not duplicates. Same-title/same-date pulses in Inbox tagged as a Delegation or DB Pulse are also legitimate — one copy lands per assignee before triage. That exception only holds while still in Inbox; if the same pair rides untriaged into Today or This Week, it becomes a real duplicate.
10. Automations — Items move by status change, not drag-and-drop.Self-check: do statuses and groups line up, or is something dragged into the wrong place?

The 2 bonus criteria (5 / 3 / 0 points — no partial credit on a miss)

11. Definition of Done — Done items carry a note on what was accomplished.
12. No Past-Due Items — Nothing in Today or This Week is past its due date.

Scoring reminder: All 60 points count toward one total. A perfect board scores 60. Green = 90%+, and everything below 60% means the board needs a reset.

The 5 highest-impact habits

  1. Set a LOW on every Today item before your sync.
  2. Tag the Project column on QG and DB pulses first, then the rest.
  3. Keep your Velocity pulse active — never mark it Done.
  4. Defer only to Monday dates.
  5. Post a one-line update on your critical project pulses so the board tells the story.
✅ Stage 2 checkpoint

Open your real board and self-score all 12 criteria. Bring your score to your Stage 3 walkthrough.

Stage 3 · Week 2

Lead the Sync

Run a sync-up that scores well on the Meeting Management criteria.

The sync-up agenda (in order)

  1. Velocity check — Review the team's pace. Happiness pulse is acknowledged here.
  2. Sort by priority — Critical on top, super-low at the bottom.
  3. Hold for Sync review — Focus on high-level and QG-value pulses. Don't cover every single item.
  4. Review This Week — Confirm dates, LOWs, and priorities.
  5. Review Today — Keep it tight and realistic.
  6. Inbox cleanup — Assign, date, prioritize, and clear.

Timing and flow

  • 5–10 min warm-up to connect and set the tone.
  • ~10 min per manager presenting their board.
  • 5–10 min for the BM to wrap Inbox and scorecard.
  • Rule of thumb: if a topic turns into a big discussion, table it and follow up 1:1 after the sync.
  • Present order: Field Manager → Flex Manager → Operations Manager → Branch Manager. Release FMs/Flex early if they need to get to on-sites.

What gets graded in a Full Eval (Meeting Management)

  • Pre-sync board prep done, RM alerted when ready
  • Agenda flow followed
  • Time kept to ~10 min per person
  • Big discussions tabled, followed up after
  • Tone engaged and constructive
  • Managers released early when appropriate
  • Velocity reviewed (with the Happiness pulse called out)
  • No drag-and-drop during the sync — status changes only
✅ Stage 3 checkpoint

Run a mock sync using the practice board. Your RM (or a peer) scores you on the 8 Meeting Management items. A passing certification is a Board Hygiene score of 90%+ plus a clean mock sync.

Certification

You're certified once you can:

  1. Pass all three stage checkpoints.
  2. Score your live board 90%+ on Board Hygiene two weeks running.
  3. Lead a mock sync that scores Green on the Meeting Management items.

Recertification runs on the same path whenever the rubric materially changes.
