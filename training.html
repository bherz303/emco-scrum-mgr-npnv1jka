Stage 1 · Day 1–2

Learn the Board

Understand what the board is, how work flows through it, and the vocabulary behind the scorecard.

Why we use a Scrum board

Your Monday board is your hammer and chisel. There is only so much time in a day, and the board is how we decide what actually gets worked, who owns it, and whether we're moving fast enough. A clean board isn't busywork — it's the difference between reacting to your week and running it.

Why this is a daily practice

Board hygiene isn't a once-a-week chore — it's daily. The goal is a board that's organized every day with the prioritized work you actually need to move, so you stay productive across the week on the numbers and projects you own.

The weekly rhythm:

  1. Monday: full plate. Today and This Week carry everything that needs attention for the week.
  2. Tuesday–Friday: work it down. Each day you knock things out, clearing Today and This Week item by item.
  3. By week's end: empty. Today and This Week are empty, and everything you touched has moved into Hold for Sync Up.
  4. Next Monday: sync and reset. That empty state is what lets you walk in, actually track last week's velocity and progress, get synced up, and start the next week clean.

Why it matters: skip this daily commitment and the board stops reflecting reality — you lose track of your own work. Left unchecked, that's not just messy, it's a failure in the role. Building the habit of daily organization is the real goal of this training; everything else in this module exists to support it.

The groups and how work flows

Work moves through groups in a predictable cycle. Learn this flow — most board problems come from items sitting in the wrong group.

  • Inbox — New/recurring, unorganized items land here. At sync or huddle: decide owner, next action, due date, priority. These get moved into your current work week, or deferred again if not a priority. The inbox should be checked and cleared daily. Nothing should sit in the inbox for more than 5 days — it is stale and mismanaged at that point.
  • Today — What you are working on today. If you're not touching it today, it doesn't belong here.
  • This Week — Work happening this week, each item carrying the projected day of focus. When that day arrives, you change the status to "Today" and it gets moved into your Today group. It's okay if you don't know the exact day of the week you need to work on it — simply put it for Wednesday, and when that day comes you can pick it up or push it to Thursday or Friday if it's not a priority yet.
  • Later / Deferred — Not this week. Every deferred item must carry a future Monday date — that triggers the automation to pull it back into Inbox before your next sync.
  • Hold for Sync Up — Where "Done" items for that week rest until reviewed at the next sync-up. Items here may be done for the week but may need to be picked back up the following week — that gets determined at the weekly sync-up meeting.
  • Done — Completed or sufficiently progressed task. This means it is fully done and you won't be picking it back up — the status should say "Synced."

This flow is automated, not manual. Changing an item's status is what moves it — you never drag a pulse between groups. Setting status to Today, This Week, Deferred, or Done automatically relocates the item, and status → Today also stamps today's date. Drag-and-drop bypasses this and is exactly what the Automations criterion is grading against.

Try it on the Mock Practice Board: duplicate the main board, rename it your test board. Then change any item's status and watch it jump groups on its own.

The Fibonacci LOW scale

We're bad at estimating time, so we use relative sizing instead. Daily Level of Work (DLOW) uses the Fibonacci scale: 1 = 15 min or less · 2 = 30–45 min · 3 = 1–2 hrs · 5 = 3–4 hrs · 8 = 5–6 hrs · 13 = 7–8 hrs · 21 = 9–12 hrs.

Daily LOW (DLOW) must always be one of these numbers. Weekly LOW (WLOW) is a cumulative total — it does not have to be Fibonacci. Target DLOW is 15–20 per person per day. If Today adds up to 40+, you're overloaded — you need to triage and reprioritize/reorganize. If you're sitting with an 8 DLOW, you haven't weighted the task correctly, or you're not filling your day with the appropriate amount of work.

QG Value, Velocity, and the Happiness pulse

  • QG Value — The point value on your Quarterly Goal pulses (your "big rocks"), usually matching Weekly LOW for that goal. Keeps your most important work visible above day-to-day noise.
  • Velocity — WLOW plus QG Value gives your pace for the week. Reviewed every sync.
  • Happiness pulse — Every team member should have one pulse that, when completed, makes them happy for the week. This typically should be tied to a critical task, a QG pulse, or something you'll be genuinely happy to get done and off your plate so you can get back to your big rocks. Visible in Today or This Week.
Example — a Velocity Check pulse's weekly update

Velocity = 88 (WLOW total)
QG Value = 20 (WLOW total for all QG pulses worked on that week)
Happiness = 5 (Very happy with work week)

  4. What is your target DLOW per day?
Stage 2 · Day 3–5

Work the Board

Keep your board scoring Green against all 12 board hygiene criteria.

This stage maps directly to the scorecard. Each item below is what a grader looks for — learn to self-check before your board is ever graded.

The 10 core criteria (5 / 3 / 1 points each)

1. LOW Values — Every Today item has a valid Fibonacci DLOW.Self-check: scan Today — is any DLOW blank or non-Fibonacci (like 4 or 6)? One exception: the Velocity Check pulse is allowed DLOW 0 — it's a tracking pulse, not a worked task. That exception applies only to that pulse; a real task with DLOW 0 is still a miss.
2. Pulse Titles — Titles name the next action with enough context. Recognized shorthand ("ESP", "Dispatch", "Courtesy Texts") is fine.Self-check: would a teammate know what to do from the title alone?
3. Later Group Dates — Every deferred item has a future Monday date.Self-check: scroll to the Later group and check the date column, or sort Deferred by date — any past dates or non-Mondays?
4. QG Pulses & Happiness Visible — At least one QG Live pulse is in Today/This Week, and a Happiness pulse is present in either.Self-check: are all of your QG pulses parked in Deferred/Later where nobody sees them and they are not being prioritized?
5. QG Value on QG Pulses — Your Quarterly Goal pulses have the QG Value column filled in.Self-check: any QG Live pulse with a blank QG Value?
6. Inbox Age — Nothing untriaged older than 5 days.Self-check: review the Inbox group — anything stale? Check every item, not just the first one you spot. Freshly dropped items (today or yesterday) are not a violation, but may need attention.
7. Project Column — QG and DB Pulse items are always tagged; overall at least half the active board is tagged so assign the project to the pulses it makes sense.Self-check: are your QG and DB pulses labeled correctly, and do other pulses need their project assigned, like "Hiring," "Fleet," "Event Planning," etc.?
8. Updates with Context — Critical ongoing-project pulses (QG, fleet repair/maintenance, customer issues, employee tracking) carry current progress notes. Simple task placeholders don't need updates.Self-check: does each ongoing-project pulse have an up-to-date note on where it stands? Could someone else pick up this task without asking you for a status update?
9. No Duplicates — One pulse per task.Note: multiple sales blocks on different days are intentional, not duplicates. Same-title/same-date pulses in Inbox tagged as a Delegation or DB Pulse are also legitimate — one copy lands per assignee before triage. That exception only holds while still in Inbox; if the same pair rides untriaged into Today or This Week, it becomes a real duplicate. If you have duplicates, combine the content into one pulse and archive the redundant pulse.
10. Automations — Items move by status change, not drag-and-drop.Self-check: do statuses and groups line up, or is something dragged into the wrong place?

The 2 bonus criteria (5 / 3 / 0 points — no partial credit on a miss)

11. Definition of Done — Pulses have a clear description of what is going to be completed that day or week in order to change the status of the pulse to "Done."
12. No Past-Due Items — Nothing in Today or This Week is past its due date.

Scoring reminder: All 60 points count toward one total. A perfect board scores 60. Green = 90%+, and everything below 60% means the board needs a reset.

The 5 highest-impact habits

  1. Set a LOW on every Today item before your sync.
  2. Tag the Project column on QG and DB pulses first, then the rest.
  3. Keep your Velocity pulse active — never mark it Done.
  4. Defer only to Monday dates.
  5. Post a one-line update on your critical project pulses so the board tells the story.
✅ Stage 2 checkpoint

Open your real board and self-score all 12 criteria. Bring your score to your Stage 3 walkthrough.

Stage 3 · Week 2

Lead the Sync

Run a sync-up that scores well on the Meeting Management criteria.

The sync-up agenda (in order)

  1. Velocity check — Review the team's pace. Happiness pulse is acknowledged here.
  2. Sort by priority — Critical on top, super-low at the bottom.
  3. Hold for Sync review — Focus on high-level and QG-value pulses. Don't cover every single item.
  4. Review This Week — Confirm dates, LOWs, and priorities.
  5. Review Today — Keep it tight and realistic.
  6. Inbox cleanup — Assign, date, prioritize, and clear.

Timing and flow

  • 5–10 min warm-up to connect and set the tone.
  • ~10 min per manager presenting their board.
  • 5–10 min for the BM to wrap Inbox and scorecard.
  • Rule of thumb: if a topic turns into a big discussion, table it and follow up 1:1 after the sync.
  • Present order: Field Manager → Flex Manager → Operations Manager → Branch Manager. Release FMs/Flex early if they need to get to on-sites.

What gets graded in a Full Eval (Meeting Management)

  • Pre-sync board prep done, RM alerted when ready
  • Agenda flow followed
  • Time kept to ~10 min per person
  • Big discussions tabled, followed up after
  • Tone engaged and constructive
  • Managers released early when appropriate
  • Velocity reviewed (with the Happiness pulse called out)
  • No drag-and-drop during the sync — status changes only
✅ Stage 3 checkpoint

Run a mock sync using the practice board. Your RM (or a peer) scores you on the 8 Meeting Management items. A passing certification is a Board Hygiene score of 90%+ plus a clean mock sync.

Certification

You're certified once you can:

  1. Pass all three stage checkpoints.
  2. Score your live board 90%+ on Board Hygiene two weeks running.
  3. Lead a mock sync that scores Green on the Meeting Management items.

Recertification runs on the same path whenever the rubric materially changes.

Staying Certified

Recheck Cadence & Recertification

Certification isn't a one-time badge. Here's how your board stays that way.

Maintenance cadence

A full board (hygiene + meeting management) runs once a month, led by the BM and supervised by the RM. Score tracked on the scoreboard.

Weekly hygiene checks are done once a week, at random. This works to make sure boards are being maintained and not just organized at sync-up and then forgotten in the middle of the week.

Flags for retraining/recertification (individual or group)

  • Back-to-back weeks of a sub-70 score means retraining is assigned.
  • If the branch's overall score for the month drops below a 70, retraining is assigned.

If retraining is assigned twice in a quarter, redirect/warnings are issued to the team, and retraining/recertification must be completed with the RM.
